the human computer

the idea behind digital computers may be explained by saying that these machines are intended to carry out any operations which could be done by a human computer. the human computer is supposed to be following ﬁxed rules; he has no authority to deviate from them in any detail. we may suppose that these rules are supplied in a book, which is altered whenever he is put on to a new job. he has also an unlimited supply of paper on which he does his calculations. Alan Turing, 1950.

(taken from Boaz Barak and Sanjeev Arora, 2009 chapter 1)
